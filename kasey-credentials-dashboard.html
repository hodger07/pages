Started: June 2015. Electrician. Age 16. Licensed: Residential Wireman. Age 18. A year after high school graduation. Five years on the tools. Katy High School. No college. Never needed it. Everything I know, I learned by doing. That's not a philosophy — it's just what happened.

Why this version:

The facts speak. No editorializing, no performed vulnerability, no copywriter fingerprints. Just a timeline of what he did and when he did it. The staccato format forces the reader to do the math themselves — "wait, he was licensed at 18? Partner in his mid-20s?" The discovery is hers, not his.

"My parents built" — warmer than "my dad built." Includes mom. Signals family values without saying "family values."

"Mid 20's" — less chest-poundy than exact ages. Confident people don't need to be precise about their accomplishments. They know.

The closer: "Everything I know, I learned by doing. That's not a philosophy — it's just what happened." Quiet. Real. No mic drop — just truth.

What the Bio Intentionally Leaves Out

The jet. "I fly planes" — doesn't say which one. The HondaJet is a date-2 revelation.

The Congressional Record. It's in his credentials section. She'll find it when she clicks. The About doesn't need to say it.

His dad's name. The company page links to Richard Hodge's profile. She'll connect the dots herself — and the discovery feels better than being told.

His grief. ALS, Michael, ibogaine — none of it belongs on LinkedIn. That depth comes in person, when it's earned.

Revenue or dollar figures. "30-some trucks" implies scale without stating numbers. Let her imagine.

Anything about dating. This is a LinkedIn profile that happens to be attractive. Not a dating profile pretending to be LinkedIn. If she can see the strategy, the strategy failed.
